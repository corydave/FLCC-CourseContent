^^ READING - Chapter 3 - Math

The reading for this chapter is [Chapter 3 - Math](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1yCS7eqh9uWHzliIbqoKRV_Z4I7pZ2kv8WR6rOMINaoY/). I always find that readers of this chapter get a kick out of how numbers interact in Java - it's quite unlike what we expect in the real world. 

For instance, `5 / 2` in the real world is `2.5`, but in Java it is `2`. If you take an integer (a whole number) and divide it by an integer, you will get an integer. If you wanted `2.5`, you should have done introduced a decimal into the expression:

* `5.0 / 2`
* `5 / 2.0`
* `5.0 / 2.0`
* `1.0 * 5 / 2`

*Interestingly, because of the order of operations, `5 / 2 * 1.0` would give you `2.0`!!!*


<div style="clear:both;"></div>


In addition to the normal arithmetic operators (addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) we will be exploring ***modulus*** (which is really like the remainder when dividing two numbers).

We'll look at some ways to generate random numbers, casting, and overflow errors (which can be bad news for you because bad actors look for overflow errors as an entry point into your software!).
