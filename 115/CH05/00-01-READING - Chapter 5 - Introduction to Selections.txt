^^ READING - Chapter 5 - Introduction to Selections

The reading for this chapter is [Chapter 5 - Introduction to Selections](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1NgGQXrp_lGwuIDEWc8bR9x4sLr-3-wjxJrFZkakmUTY/). In this chapter we'll start looking at `if` statements. To me, programming with *if* statements (we call them *conditionals*) is fairly close to the way we talk. Sure, it looks complicated when you introduce brackets, but logically `if` statements are organized the same way we think about them in every day life:

```java

if (age >= 35) {
  System.out.println("You are old enough to run for President.");
}

```

Things can get a bit more complicated once we start adding multiple conditions and also introduce `then` statements - but don't worry about that now. Just try to wrap your head around using the plain 'ole `if` statements for now.
