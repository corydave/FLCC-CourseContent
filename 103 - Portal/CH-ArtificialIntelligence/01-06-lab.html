LAB - Using AI Tools to Learn

Description

Go back to your reflection notes from the start of this chapter, where you wrote down a topic you're an expert in, something you know absolutely nothing about, and something you're currently learning about. Now pick one of those three topics - lock in!

NOTE: I truly have no preference on what you choose for the topic. Do what works for you. If it helps you to pick something that helps you study for a final exam, learn a new recipe, learn how to save money, learn more about that one random topic you've thought about since 2nd grade like your favorite dinosaur - ANYTHING! Pick whatever YOU want!

You'll pick two (or more) AI tools to learn more about this topic. The format of the generated content is your choice, based on the tools you'll select. You'll compare the two tools to see which one helped you more, the types of results they produce, and any extra learning that happened as a result of your experience!

You can choose to take this in your own direction as long as you're comparing two tools, but here are some ideas...

  • Something you're an expert in
    • REALLY analyze your AI results since you're an expert - check to see if there are any hallucinations
  • Something you know absolutely nothing about
    • Which tool was most helpful for you to learn as a totally new beginner?
  • Something you're currently learning about
    • Share what you know so far, what you'd like to learn next

Which tools should you use?

Any two of your choice. If you're brand new to using AI, the most popular are ChatGPT, Gemini (Google account), Claude, and Microsoft Copilot. You can use Microsoft Copilot with your FLCC login credentials. There's also Duck.ai which does not require a login or save your conversation - you can switch between chat models in the top left corner. They're sometimes older models but that's okay! If you're looking for something different, go to the FLCC Resource Catalog!

Specifications

  • Submit a summary of your experience - What did you learn? What information was helpful? Which tool(s) do you prefer most? Why?

  • Submit a PDF.   Four (4) pages max.  One page might be totally fine - I included up to four in case you have several screenshots or info you want to share. 

    • If you've never created a PDF, write your response in a Google Doc or Word Doc or other editor. You can include text, screenshots, drawings, etc. Then Export to PDF! 
    • It's not required to submit a hyperlink to the conversation, but if you have an interesting conversation that you'd like to share, you can include the hyperlink.
